How to make money with HTML5 apps

The first thing you have to think about it is, what is the best distribution option for this app.
You need to make your app available for download and purchase. Furthermore people have to find your app.

So, what are the distribution option for Mobile HTML5 apps?
You can't distribute an HTML5 app on Google Play, App Store, Amazon, and other App Stores where only native apps are allowed.

The good news is, there are a lot of other options for HTML5 apps.

  1. OpenAppMkt.com
    OpenAppMkt is an App store for HTML5 apps only. iPhone, iPad and Android user can download apps from here. But it's not like Google Play or App store where you upload your package, you submit an URL where the HTML5 app is hosted and can be downloaded. This means, you need a webserver elsewhere to host your app.
  2. Facebook
    Apps for Facebook are in general made in HTML5.
    The step by step description from the Facebook devolper site is easy to understand. And like on OpenAppMkt, you need to host your HTML5 app on a webserver your choice and submit to Facebook the URL to it. Facebook is maybe not the first choice for every kind of app but one plus one Facebook's site is, that all apps are available for all smartphones with HTML5 capability.
  3. Firefox Marketplace
    A new kid in town. Apps for Firefox Marketplace can be made available for Firefox OS, Android and Windows 7 devices. The apps for the Firefox Marketplace are based on HTML5can be packed with the Firefox OS SDK for upload to the Firefox Marketplace (only avalilable for Firefox OS apps right now).Everybody else to link to the server where the app is hosted. This server can not be any webserver, and github-pages are supported. Very good and understable description.
  4. AppsFuel.com
    AppsFuel is also an App Store for HTML5 apps (mobile and desktop). To distribute your app you have to add to your app "An Open Web App manifest" (the same you need for the Firefox Marketplace). Your submission is made by register the URL of the app or the URL of the Open Web App manifest file. I have seen apps for iphone, Android and Windows. It's the developers choice which platform is supported.
  5. Google App Engine
    On Google's App Engine you can host HTML5 apps. The HTML5 app has to be integrated into a Web App based on Java or Python. I'm using Google App Engine for all my HTML5 Mobile Apps.
    I find it quite easy to use and it's free. If you don't connect your app to OpenAppMkt, Facebook or Firefox Marketplace, it can be downloaded from every smartphone with HTML5 capabilities from the original URL.
  6. A standard webserver of your choice, Amazon, Linode, GoDaddy, ... You need to have webserver admistration knowledge to make it work. But it's of course the most individual and flexible solution.

Now your app is in place, people are able to download it.

How can people find your app?
  1. In most cases it's a good idea to start with Facebook. Let your Facebook friends know about your app. Another option to use Facebook is, to give your app his own Facebook page and invite people to this page.
  2. Infos and Link on your homepage.
  3. Send infos about your app to App review sites.
  4. Make for example stickers with QR Code like this and put them on appropriate places:


Be creative.

Finally, people find your app.

How to get payed?
  1. On OpenAppMkt, you can set a price for your app like on Google Play or App Store.
  2. If your app is on a webserver or on Google App Engine, you're free to use what you want. One of the options here is PayPal which can be integrated in Standard HTML Sites or Google Sites and inside your app.
    You need to upgrade your PayPal account to Premier (free) or Business.
  3. Another payment system for purchasing apps and in-app payments is ZooZ. ZooZ integreates very well.
  4. It is also possible to integrate Ads. Right now I'm using Banner from Leadbolt. Leadbolt has two options for HTML5 mobile web apps. App Wall and Banner. Banner works fine in an app with jQuery, App Wall don't.
  5. inMobi
  6. Adsense and Adsense for web games (new)
  7. Amobee inside of AppsFuel
  8. AdGear

I'm sure that big players like Admob will follow and come up with a solution for HTML5 apps soon. I will update this chapter if something new is showing up. Stay tuned.
